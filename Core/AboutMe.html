About Me.

I am a Computer Engineering student at Georgia Tech. I realized it was my calling while attending my local career-oriented school as I was taking classes like cybersecurity, embedded computing, and engineering principles. My interests were further confirmed as I joined and led my local VEX Robotics organization. It initiated a spark in me to pursue engineering and programming in team environments through clubs, research, and personal projects.

I was born in Lawrenceville Georgia, but I grew up in Newnan, Georgia. I learned to love music through piano and nature through hiking, camping, and backpacking. My family got a dog and 2 cats which eventually became 3 dogs, so I have fallen in love with our furry friends. I also went to school, fell in love with math, and proceeded to join my High School's VEX Robotics Competition team. There, I became a team leader and learned how to effectively lead a team. I especially learned how to design a robot to solve an annually unique challenge and code a program for that robot. I also learned important leading skills such as how to teach new members to use equipment and design parts on their own. Since then, I have loved engineering and love to watch YouTube videos in my free time on different engineers solving cool problems and seeing how they solve the issue. I've also done many small projects of my own to fulfill this desire and interest of mine in engineering.


During my primary education, I also joined the Boy Scouts of America (BSA) and joined community service organizations such as Beta Club and National Honor Society. Through these organizations and my personal free time, I was able to perform many acts of community service and make friends while doing so. My largest project being my Eagle Scout Project where I directed a community service project myself from start to finish where we built a putting green for a local assisted living community to help them move while having fun.
